Focaccia


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Dough:

      • 4 cups all-purpose flour
      • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
      • 1 1/2 cups warm water
      • 1 teaspoon sugar
      • 2 teaspoons salt
      • 1/4 cup olive oil

    • For the Topping:

      • 2-3 tablespoons olive oil
      • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary (chopped)
      • Sea salt (to taste)
      • Optional: Sliced olives, cherry tomatoes

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Dough:

      In a small bowl, dissolve sugar and yeast in warm water. Let it sit until frothy (5-10 minutes).

      In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead to form a soft, elastic dough.

      Cover with a damp cloth and let it rise for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.


    • Shape the Dough:

      Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease a baking sheet with olive oil.

      Transfer the dough to the sheet and spread it out evenly. Use your fingers to create dimples in the surface.


    • Add the Toppings:

      Drizzle olive oil over the dough. Sprinkle with rosemary and sea salt. Add optional toppings like olives or tomatoes.


    • Bake:

      Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly.


    • Serve:

      Slice and serve warm or at room temperature.

Gnocchi Di Patate


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Dough:

      • 2 pounds (1 kg) potatoes (Russet or Yukon Gold)
      • 1 cup all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
      • 1 egg yolk
      • Salt (to taste)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Potatoes:

      Boil the potatoes with their skins on until fork-tender. Drain, peel, and mash while still warm.


    • Make the Dough:

      Mix mashed potatoes, flour, egg yolk, and salt to form a soft dough. Avoid overworking to prevent toughness.


    • Shape the Gnocchi:

      Divide dough into small portions. Roll into thin ropes and cut into 1-inch pieces. Use a fork to create ridges if desired.


    • Cook:

      Boil gnocchi in salted water until they float (2-3 minutes). Remove and serve with your favorite sauce.

Pizza Margherita


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Dough:

      • 3 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
      • 1 teaspoon salt
      • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
      • 1 cup warm water
      • 1 tablespoon olive oil

    • For the Topping:

      • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
      • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella (sliced)
      • Fresh basil leaves
      • 2 tablespoons olive oil
      • Salt (to taste)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Dough:

      In a small bowl, mix yeast with warm water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.

      In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add yeast mixture and olive oil, kneading to form a smooth dough.

      Cover and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.


    • Shape the Dough:

      Preheat the oven to 475°F (250°C) and place a pizza stone or tray inside to heat.

      Roll out the dough on a floured surface into a circle and transfer to parchment paper.


    • Add the Topping:

      Spread tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border. Add sliced mozzarella and a few basil leaves. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle salt.


    • Bake:

      Carefully transfer the pizza to the hot stone or tray. Bake for 7-10 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese bubbly.


    • Serve:

      Garnish with fresh basil and serve hot.

Risotto


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Risotto:

      • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
      • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock (warmed)
      • 1/2 cup dry white wine
      • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
      • 2 tablespoons olive oil
      • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
      • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)
      • Optional: Saffron or mushrooms

  • Recipe:


    • Sauté the Onion:

      Heat olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until soft.


    • Toast the Rice:

      Add Arborio rice to the pan and stir until coated and lightly toasted (2-3 minutes).


    • Add Wine:

      Pour in white wine and cook, stirring, until it's mostly absorbed.


    • Cook the Risotto:

      Add warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait for each addition to be absorbed before adding more.

      Continue until the rice is creamy and al dente (about 18-20 minutes).


    • Finish and Serve:

      Stir in remaining butter, Parmesan, and any additional ingredients like saffron or mushrooms. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

      Serve hot.

Tiramisu


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Layers:

      • 1 cup strong espresso (cooled)
      • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
      • 1 package (7 ounces) ladyfingers

    • For the Cream:

      • 3 large egg yolks
      • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
      • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese
      • 1 cup heavy cream (whipped to stiff peaks)
      • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    • For the Topping:

      • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder
      • Optional: Dark chocolate shavings

  • Recipe:


    • Make the Coffee Mixture:

      Mix cooled espresso with coffee liqueur (if using). Set aside.


    • Prepare the Cream:

      In a bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar over a double boiler until pale and creamy. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

      Fold in mascarpone cheese, whipped cream, and vanilla extract until smooth.


    • Layer the Tiramisu:

      Dip ladyfingers briefly in the coffee mixture and arrange them in a single layer in a dish.

      Spread half the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers. Repeat layers, ending with cream on top.


    • Chill:

      Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for the flavors to meld.


    • Serve:

      Dust with cocoa powder and garnish with chocolate shavings before serving.
