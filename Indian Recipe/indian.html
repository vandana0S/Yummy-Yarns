Aloo Parantha


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Dough:

      • 2 cups whole wheat flour
      • Water (as needed)
      • Salt (to taste)
      • 1 tablespoon oil

    • For the Filling:

      • 4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and mashed)
      • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
      • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
      • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
      • 1 teaspoon garam masala
      • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
      • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
      • Salt (to taste)
      • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (for cooking)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Dough:

      In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a soft, pliable dough. Add a tablespoon of oil and knead again. Cover and let it rest for about 20-30 minutes.


    • Prepare the Filling:

      Boil the potatoes until tender. Peel and mash them in a bowl. Add green chilies, grated ginger, cumin seeds, garam masala, red chili powder, coriander leaves, and salt to the mashed potatoes. Mix well.


    • Assemble the Parathas:

      Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Roll out one ball into a small circle using a rolling pin. Place a spoonful of the potato filling in the center of the circle. Bring the edges together to seal the filling inside and form a ball again. Gently roll out the stuffed ball into a flat circle, being careful not to let the filling spill out.


    • Cook the Parathas

      Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat. Place the rolled-out paratha on the tawa and cook until bubbles start to form. Flip the paratha and apply a little oil or ghee on the cooked side. Flip again and apply oil or ghee on the other side. Cook until both sides are golden brown and crispy.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or butter.

Biryani


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Rice:

      • 2 cups basmati rice
      • 4 cups of Water
      • Salt (to taste)
      • 2-3 cloves
      • 2-3 green cardamoms
      • 1 bay leaf
      • 1-inch cinnamon stick

    • For the vegetables:

      • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, cauliflower, potatoes, cut into small pieces)
      • 2 tbsp oil

    • For the Masala:

      • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
      • 2 large tomatoes (chopped)
      • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
      • 2 green chilies (slit)
      • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
      • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
      • 1 tsp cumin seeds
      • 1 tsp turmeric powder
      • 1 tsp red chili powder
      • 1 tsp garam masala
      • 1 tsp biryani masala (optional)
      • Salt (to taste)
      • 2 tbsp yogurt

    • For the Layering:

      • A few strands of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)
      • 2 tbsp fried onions (optional)
      • 2 tbsp ghee

  • Recipe:


    • Preparing the Rice:

      Wash the basmati rice until the water runs clear. Soak for 30 minutes, then drain. Boil 4 cups of water with cloves, cardamoms, bay leaf, cinnamon, and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until 70% done (rice should still be firm). Drain and set aside.


    • Cooking the Vegetables:

      Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan and sautÃ© the mixed vegetables until slightly tender. Remove and set aside.


    • Making the Masala:

      In the same pan, heat 2 tbsp oil and fry the onions until golden brown. Remove some for garnishing. Add cumin seeds, ginger-garlic paste, and green chilies. SautÃ© for 2 minutes. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and biryani masala. Cook until the oil separates. Add the yogurt, mix well, and cook for 2 minutes. Add the sautÃ©ed vegetables, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Cook for 5 minutes.


    • Layering the Biryani:

      In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of half-cooked rice. Add the vegetable masala over the rice. Top with the remaining rice. Drizzle saffron milk, ghee, and fried onions on top. Cover with a tight lid or seal with dough to lock in the steam.


    • Cooking the Biryani:

      Place the pot on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.

Chhole Bhature


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Chhole:

      • 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight)
      • 2 tbsp oil
      • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
      • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
      • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
      • 1 tsp cumin seeds
      • 1 tsp turmeric powder
      • 1 tsp red chili powder
      • 1 tsp garam masala
      • 1 tsp chhole masala (optional)
      • 1 bay leaf
      • 2-3 cloves
      • Salt (to taste)
      • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)

    • For the Bhature:

      • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
      • 1/4 cup semolina (optional, for crispiness)
      • 1/2 cup yogurt
      • 1/4 tsp baking soda
      • 1/4 tsp sugar
      • Salt (to taste)
      • Water (as needed)
      • Oil (for frying)

  • Recipe:


    • Preparing the Chhole:

      Pressure cook the soaked chickpeas with 3 cups of water, a pinch of salt, and a bay leaf for 5-6 whistles or until soft. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds, cloves, and ginger-garlic paste. SautÃ© until fragrant. Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown. Add tomato puree, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and chhole masala. Cook until oil separates. Add the cooked chickpeas along with the cooking water. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the gravy thickens. Garnish with coriander leaves.


    • Preparing the Bhature Dough:

      In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Add yogurt and knead into a soft dough, adding water as needed. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1-2 hours.


    • Making the Bhature:

      Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll them into round or oval shapes. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Carefully slide a rolled dough into the hot oil. Fry on medium-high heat, pressing lightly with a slotted spoon, until the bhature puffs up and turns golden. Remove and drain excess oil on paper towels.


    • Serving:

      Serve the hot chhole with fluffy bhature, along with a side of onion rings, green chilies, and lemon wedges.

Dal Makhni


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Dal:

      • 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal)
      • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
      • 4 cups water (for pressure cooking)
      • Salt (to taste)

    • For the Masala:

      • 2 tbsp butter
      • 1 tbsp oil
      • 2 large onions (finely chopped)
      • 2 large tomatoes (pureed)
      • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
      • 2 green chilies (slit)
      • 1/4 cup fresh cream
      • 1 tsp turmeric powder
      • 1 tsp red chili powder
      • 1 tsp garam masala
      • 1 tsp cumin seeds
      • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
      • Salt (to taste)

    • For Garnishing:

      • 1 tbsp butter
      • 1 tbsp fresh cream
      • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

  • Recipe:


    • Preparing the Dal:

      Wash the black lentils and red kidney beans thoroughly. Soak them in water for 6-8 hours or overnight. Drain and add 4 cups of water with a pinch of salt in a pressure cooker. Cook for 6-8 whistles or until soft.


    • Making the Masala:

      Heat butter and oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sautÃ© for 2 minutes. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates.


    • Combining the Dal and Masala:

      Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the masala. Mix well and simmer for 15-20 minutes on low heat. Add cream and garam masala, and cook for another 5 minutes.


    • Garnishing the Dal Makhni:

      Add butter and fresh cream on top before serving. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.


Shahi Paneer


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Paneer:

      • 200 g paneer (cubed)
      • 2 tbsp ghee or oil

    • For the Gravy:

      • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
      • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
      • 1/4 cup cashews (soaked and ground into a paste)
      • 1/2 cup fresh cream
      • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
      • 2 green chilies (slit)
      • 1 tsp turmeric powder
      • 1 tsp red chili powder
      • 1 tsp garam masala
      • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
      • Salt (to taste)
      • 1 cup water
      • 2 tbsp coriander leaves (chopped, for garnish)

    • For the Tempering:

      • 1 tbsp butter
      • 1 tsp cumin seeds
      • 1 bay leaf
      • 2-3 cloves
      • 1-inch cinnamon stick

  • Recipe:


    • Preparing the Paneer:

      Heat 2 tbsp ghee or oil in a pan and lightly sautÃ© the paneer cubes until golden on the edges. Remove and set aside.


    • Making the Gravy:

      In the same pan, add butter and the tempering spices (cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, and cinnamon). SautÃ© for 30 seconds.

      Add the chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown. Then, add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute.

      Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.

      Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, cardamom powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2 minutes.

      Stir in the cashew paste and water. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

      Add the fresh cream and mix thoroughly. Cook for another 2 minutes.


    • Adding the Paneer:

      Gently add the sautÃ©ed paneer cubes to the gravy. Simmer for 3-5 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.


    • Garnishing and Serving:

      Turn off the heat and garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
