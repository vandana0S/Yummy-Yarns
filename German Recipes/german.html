Bratkartoffeln (German Fried Potatoes)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 4 medium potatoes (boiled and sliced)
      • 2 tablespoons butter
      • 1 tablespoon oil
      • 1 onion (finely chopped)
      • 2 slices bacon (optional, diced)
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)
      • Chives (for garnish)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Potatoes:

      Boil the potatoes until tender. Cool and slice into even rounds.


    • Cook the Potatoes:

      Heat butter and oil in a pan. Add potato slices in a single layer and fry until golden and crispy on both sides.


    • Cook the Add-Ons:

      In the same pan, sauté onions and bacon (if using) until golden and fragrant.


    • Combine:

      Toss the crispy potatoes with the onion and bacon mixture. Season with salt and pepper.


    • Serve:

      Garnish with chives and serve hot as a side dish.

Bratwurst (German Sausage)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 2 pounds ground pork
      • 1 pound ground veal or beef
      • 2 teaspoons salt
      • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
      • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
      • 1 teaspoon marjoram
      • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
      • 1/4 cup cold water
      • Sausage casings (optional)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Sausage Mixture:

      In a large bowl, combine ground pork, veal (or beef), and spices. Add cold water and mix until the mixture is smooth and sticky.


    • Stuff the Sausages:

      If using casings, soak them in water and rinse. Stuff the sausage mixture into the casings using a sausage stuffer, twisting to form links. Alternatively, form the mixture into patties.


    • Cook the Bratwurst:

      Grill, pan-fry, or bake the sausages until fully cooked, turning occasionally for even browning.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with mustard, sauerkraut, or bread rolls.

Currywurst (German Curry Sausage)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 4 bratwursts
      • 1 cup ketchup
      • 2 teaspoons curry powder
      • 1 teaspoon paprika
      • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
      • 1 teaspoon sugar
      • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
      • 1 tablespoon oil (for frying)
      • Fries or bread rolls (for serving)

  • Recipe:


    • Cook the Bratwurst:

      Grill or fry the bratwurst until golden and cooked through. Slice into bite-sized pieces.


    • Prepare the Sauce:

      In a small pan, mix ketchup, curry powder, paprika, Worcestershire sauce, sugar, and cayenne pepper. Heat gently until combined.


    • Assemble:

      Pour the curry sauce over the sliced bratwurst and sprinkle with extra curry powder.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with fries or bread rolls.

Rouladen (German Beef Rolls)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 4 thin beef slices (about 4x8 inches)
      • 4 tablespoons mustard
      • 4 slices bacon
      • 1 onion (chopped)
      • 2 pickles (cut into strips)
      • 1 cup beef broth
      • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
      • 2 tablespoons flour
      • 2 tablespoons oil
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Rolls:

      Lay out beef slices. Spread mustard over each slice and top with bacon, chopped onions, and pickle strips. Roll tightly and secure with toothpicks or kitchen twine.


    • Brown the Rolls:

      Heat oil in a skillet and brown the beef rolls on all sides. Remove and set aside.


    • Simmer:

      Add broth and wine to the skillet, scraping the bottom. Return the rolls to the skillet and simmer, covered, for 1.5-2 hours.


    • Thicken the Sauce:

      Remove the rolls. Stir flour into the sauce to thicken, then return the rolls to coat with the sauce.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with potatoes or red cabbage.

Spätzle (German Egg Noodles)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 2 cups all-purpose flour
      • 1/2 teaspoon salt
      • 2 eggs
      • 1/2 cup milk
      • 2 tablespoons butter
      • Chives (for garnish, optional)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Dough:

      In a bowl, mix flour and salt. Add eggs and milk, stirring to form a thick batter.


    • Cook the Spätzle:

      Press the batter through a spätzle maker or colander into boiling salted water. Cook until the noodles float to the surface, then remove with a slotted spoon.


    • Sauté:

      Melt butter in a skillet and toss the cooked spätzle until lightly browned.


    • Serve:

      Serve warm, garnished with chives, as a side or main dish.
