Bœuf Bourguignon


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 1 kg beef (cut into chunks)
      • 2 cups red wine
      • 1 cup beef stock
      • 2 tablespoons flour
      • 100 g bacon (diced)
      • 2 onions (sliced)
      • 2 carrots (chopped)
      • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
      • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
      • 1 bouquet garni (herb bundle)
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)
      • 2 tablespoons butter or oil

  • Recipe:


    • Sear the Meat:

      Heat butter or oil in a pot and brown the beef. Remove and set aside.


    • Cook the Aromatics:

      Sauté bacon, onions, carrots, and garlic in the same pot. Add tomato paste and flour, stirring well.


    • Add Liquids:

      Return the beef to the pot. Add wine, beef stock, and the bouquet garni. Bring to a boil.


    • Simmer:

      Reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the meat is tender.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with potatoes or crusty bread.

Chocolate Soufflé


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 4 oz dark chocolate
      • 2 tablespoons butter (plus extra for greasing)
      • 2 tablespoons flour
      • 1/2 cup milk
      • 3 eggs (separated)
      • 1/4 cup sugar
      • Pinch of salt
      • Powdered sugar (for garnish)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Base:

      Melt chocolate and butter in a saucepan. Stir in flour, then gradually add milk. Cook until thickened. Cool slightly.


    • Whisk Egg Whites:

      Beat egg whites with sugar and salt until stiff peaks form.


    • Combine:

      Mix egg yolks into the chocolate base, then gently fold in the egg whites.


    • Bake:

      Pour the mixture into greased ramekins and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 12-15 minutes.


    • Serve:

      Dust with powdered sugar and serve immediately.

Crêpes


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 1 cup flour
      • 2 eggs
      • 1 cup milk
      • 1/2 cup water
      • 2 tablespoons melted butter
      • Pinch of salt

  • Recipe:


    • Make the Batter:

      Whisk together flour, eggs, milk, water, butter, and salt until smooth. Let rest for 30 minutes.


    • Cook:

      Heat a non-stick pan and pour a ladleful of batter. Tilt the pan to spread evenly. Cook for 1-2 minutes per side.


    • Serve:

      Fill with sweet or savory fillings like Nutella, jam, or cheese. Roll or fold and serve warm.

Croissants


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 4 cups all-purpose flour
      • 1/2 cup sugar
      • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
      • 1 cup warm milk
      • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
      • 1 teaspoon salt
      • 1 cup unsalted butter (cold, for laminating)
      • 1 egg (beaten, for egg wash)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Dough:

      Mix warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Let sit until foamy. Combine with flour, salt, and softened butter to form a dough. Knead until smooth and let it rest for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.


    • Laminating the Dough:

      Roll out the dough into a rectangle. Place the cold butter in the center, fold the dough over, and roll out. Fold into thirds and refrigerate for 30 minutes. Repeat the process 3-4 times.


    • Shape the Croissants:

      Roll out the dough into a large rectangle and cut into triangles. Roll each triangle tightly from the base to the tip to form croissants.


    • Proof:

      Place the croissants on a baking tray, cover with a clean cloth, and let them proof for 1-2 hours.


    • Bake:

      Brush with egg wash and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.


    • Serve:

      Serve warm with butter, jam, or as is!

Soupe à l’Oignon (French Onion Soup)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 4 large onions (thinly sliced)
      • 4 tablespoons butter
      • 1 tablespoon olive oil
      • 4 cups beef broth
      • 1 cup white wine
      • 1 baguette (sliced)
      • 1 cup grated Gruyère cheese
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)

  • Recipe:


    • Caramelize the Onions:

      Heat butter and oil in a pot. Add onions and cook over low heat until golden and caramelized, stirring occasionally.


    • Simmer:

      Add the wine to deglaze the pot, then pour in the beef broth. Simmer for 20-30 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.


    • Prepare the Toppings:

      Toast the baguette slices and sprinkle with Gruyère cheese.


    • Assemble:

      Ladle the soup into bowls, top with toasted bread, and sprinkle more cheese. Broil in the oven until the cheese is bubbly and golden.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot as a starter or light meal.
