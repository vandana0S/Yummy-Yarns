Erdäpfelgulasch (Potato Goulash)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
      • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
      • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
      • 1 tablespoon sweet paprika
      • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
      • 4 medium potatoes (peeled and diced)
      • 3 cups vegetable or beef stock
      • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
      • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)
      • 1/4 cup sour cream (for garnish, optional)

  • Recipe:


    • Sauté the Onions:

      Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Stir in garlic.


    • Add Spices:

      Stir in sweet and smoked paprika, ensuring it doesn't burn. Add tomato paste and caraway seeds.


    • Add Potatoes:

      Mix in the diced potatoes. Pour in stock to cover them and bring to a boil.


    • Simmer:

      Reduce the heat and simmer until potatoes are tender and the sauce thickens, about 25-30 minutes.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot, garnished with sour cream if desired, and accompanied by crusty bread.

Fleischlaberln (Austrian Meat Patties)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 500g ground beef or pork (or a mix)
      • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
      • 1 clove garlic (minced)
      • 1 egg
      • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
      • 1 teaspoon mustard
      • 1 teaspoon marjoram
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)
      • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)

  • Recipe:


    • Mix Ingredients:

      In a large bowl, combine ground meat, onion, garlic, egg, breadcrumbs, mustard, marjoram, salt, and pepper. Mix well.


    • Form Patties:

      Shape the mixture into equal-sized patties, about 1 inch thick.


    • Cook:

      Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry the patties for 4-5 minutes on each side or until cooked through.


    • Serve:

      Serve warm with mashed potatoes or a side salad.

Sachertorte (chocolate cake)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Cake:

      • 150g dark chocolate (melted)
      • 6 eggs (separated)
      • 150g unsalted butter (softened)
      • 150g sugar
      • 150g flour
      • 1/4 teaspoon salt

    • For the Topping:

      • 200g apricot jam
      • 200g dark chocolate
      • 100ml heavy cream

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Batter:

      Cream butter and sugar until fluffy. Add yolks, one at a time. Stir in melted chocolate. Whisk egg whites with salt until stiff and fold into the mixture with flour.


    • Bake:

      Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Pour batter into a greased pan and bake for 45-50 minutes. Cool completely.


    • Prepare the Topping:

      Heat jam and spread over the cake. Melt chocolate with cream and pour over the cake, smoothing it evenly.


    • Serve:

      Let the glaze set before slicing. Serve with whipped cream.

Tafelsptiz (Austrian Boiled Beef)


  • Ingredients:


    • Main Ingredients:

      • 1.5 kg beef rump or tri-tip
      • 2 liters beef stock
      • 2 carrots (peeled and chopped)
      • 1 parsley root (peeled and chopped)
      • 1 leek (sliced)
      • 1 onion (halved and roasted)
      • 1 bay leaf
      • 5 black peppercorns
      • Salt (to taste)
      • Chives (chopped, for garnish)

    • For the Accompaniments:

      • Horseradish sauce
      • Apple-horseradish puree
      • Roasted potatoes or bread dumplings

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Stock:

      Bring the beef stock to a simmer in a large pot. Add carrots, parsley root, leek, onion, bay leaf, peppercorns, and salt.


    • Cook the Beef:

      Place the beef in the stock. Simmer gently for 2-3 hours until tender, skimming any foam or fat from the surface.


    • Serve:

      Slice the beef and serve it with the broth, vegetables, and accompaniments. Garnish with chopped chives.

Viennese Apfelstrudel (Apple Strudel)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Dough:

      • 2 cups all-purpose flour
      • 1/4 teaspoon salt
      • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
      • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
      • 1 tablespoon vinegar

    • For the Filling:

      • 6 medium apples (peeled and thinly sliced)
      • 1/2 cup sugar
      • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
      • 1/4 cup raisins
      • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (toasted in butter)
      • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
      • 2 tablespoons butter (melted)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Dough:

      Mix flour and salt in a bowl. Add water, oil, and vinegar. Knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let rest for 1 hour.


    • Prepare the Filling:

      In a bowl, mix apples, sugar, cinnamon, raisins, lemon juice, and toasted breadcrumbs.


    • Roll Out the Dough:

      On a floured surface, roll the dough as thin as possible. Use a cloth to stretch it further until almost transparent.


    • Assemble:

      Spread the filling over the dough, leaving a border. Roll the dough tightly using the cloth, sealing the edges.


    • Bake:

      Place the strudel on a baking sheet. Brush with melted butter. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35-40 minutes until golden brown.


    • Serve:

      Cool slightly, slice, and serve with powdered sugar or whipped cream.
