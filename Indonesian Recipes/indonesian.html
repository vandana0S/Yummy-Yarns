Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Fried Rice:

      • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
      • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (Kecap Manis)
      • 1 tablespoon regular soy sauce
      • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
      • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
      • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
      • 1 chili (optional, finely chopped)
      • 1 egg (beaten)
      • Optional: Protein (chicken, shrimp, or tofu)


  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Ingredients:

      Ensure the rice is cooked and slightly dry for frying. Gather and chop all ingredients.


    • Cook the Egg:

      Heat a wok or skillet with oil. Add the beaten egg, scramble, and set aside.


    • Stir-fry the Base:

      Add more oil to the wok. Sauté garlic, onion, and chili until fragrant. Add your protein and stir-fry until cooked.


    • Combine and Cook:

      Add the rice to the wok, followed by the sweet soy sauce and regular soy sauce. Stir until evenly coated. Mix in the scrambled egg.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot, garnished with fried shallots, cucumber slices, or a fried egg on top.

Sate Ayam (Chicken Satay)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Satay:

      • 500g chicken breast (cut into cubes)
      • 10-12 bamboo skewers (soaked in water)

    • For the Marinade:

      • 3 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (Kecap Manis)
      • 1 tablespoon peanut butter
      • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
      • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)


  • Recipe:


    • Marinate the Chicken:

      Mix all marinade ingredients in a bowl. Add chicken cubes and coat thoroughly. Let marinate for at least 1 hour.


    • Skewer the Chicken:

      Thread the marinated chicken cubes onto the soaked bamboo skewers.


    • Grill the Satay:

      Grill the skewers over medium heat, turning occasionally, until fully cooked (about 10-12 minutes).


    • Serve:

      Serve with peanut sauce and rice cakes (Lontong).

Gado-Gado (Indonesian Salad with Peanut Sauce)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Salad:

      • 1 cup boiled potatoes (cubed)
      • 1 cup blanched green beans
      • 1 cup bean sprouts
      • 1 cucumber (sliced)
      • 1 boiled egg (sliced)
      • Optional: Fried tofu or tempeh

    • For the Peanut Sauce:

      • 1/2 cup peanut butter
      • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce
      • 1 tablespoon lime juice
      • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
      • 1 chili (optional)
      • Water (to thin the sauce)


  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Peanut Sauce:

      Blend all sauce ingredients until smooth, adding water gradually to reach the desired consistency.


    • Assemble the Salad:

      Arrange the vegetables, boiled egg, tofu, and tempeh on a plate.


    • Serve:

      Pour peanut sauce over the salad and serve with crackers or rice cakes.

Rendang (Indonesian Slow-Cooked Beef Curry)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Rendang:

      • 1 kg beef (cut into chunks)
      • 400 ml coconut milk
      • 2 lemongrass stalks (bruised)
      • 4 kaffir lime leaves
      • 2 turmeric leaves (optional)
      • 2 tablespoons tamarind juice
      • Salt (to taste)

    • For the Spice Paste:

      • 6 shallots
      • 4 garlic cloves
      • 5 red chilies
      • 1 piece galangal (peeled and sliced)
      • 1 piece ginger (peeled and sliced)
      • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
      • 1 teaspoon ground cumin


  • Recipe:


    • Make the Spice Paste:

      Blend all spice paste ingredients into a smooth mixture.


    • Sauté the Paste:

      Heat oil in a wok. Add the spice paste and sauté until fragrant.


    • Cook the Beef:

      Add beef chunks and stir to coat them with the paste. Pour in coconut milk, tamarind juice, and add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and turmeric leaves.


    • Simmer:

      Cook on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the liquid is mostly evaporated, and the beef becomes tender and caramelized.


    • Serve:

      Serve with steamed rice.

Soto Ayam (Indonesian Chicken Soup)


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Soup:

      • 500g chicken (with bones)
      • 2 liters water
      • 2 lemongrass stalks (bruised)
      • 4 kaffir lime leaves
      • 1 turmeric root (peeled and sliced)
      • Salt and pepper (to taste)

    • For the Spice Paste:

      • 4 shallots
      • 3 garlic cloves
      • 2 candlenuts (or macadamia nuts)
      • 1 piece ginger
      • 1 teaspoon ground coriander

    • For Garnish:

      • Boiled eggs (halved)
      • Fried shallots
      • Bean sprouts
      • Sliced green onions
      • Vermicelli noodles
      • Optional: Lime wedges

  • Recipe:


    • Make the Spice Paste:

      Blend all spice paste ingredients into a smooth mixture.


    • Cook the Soup Base:

      Heat oil in a pot and sauté the spice paste until fragrant. Add chicken, water, lemongrass, lime leaves, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.


    • Simmer the Soup:

      Lower the heat and simmer until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Remove the chicken, shred it, and return to the soup.


    • Prepare the Garnish:

      Boil vermicelli noodles and prepare toppings like eggs, fried shallots, and bean sprouts.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with the garnishes on top, and squeeze lime juice over if desired.
