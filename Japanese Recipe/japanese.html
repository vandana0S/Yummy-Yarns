Ramen


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Ramen Broth:

      • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
      • 2 cups water
      • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
      • 1 tablespoon miso paste
      • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
      • 1 clove garlic (minced)
      • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
      • Salt to taste

    • For the Noodles and Toppings:

      • 200g ramen noodles
      • 2 boiled eggs (halved)
      • 100g sliced cooked chicken or tofu
      • 1 sheet nori (cut into strips)
      • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
      • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
      • 1/4 cup sweet corn

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Broth:

      In a pot, heat sesame oil and sauté garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add chicken or vegetable stock, water, soy sauce, and miso paste. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes.


    • Cook the Noodles:

      Boil the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.


    • Assemble the Ramen:

      Divide the noodles into serving bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles. Top with boiled eggs, chicken or tofu, nori strips, mushrooms, sweet corn, and green onions.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot and enjoy your homemade ramen!

Sushi


  • Ingredients:


    • For Sushi Rice:

      • 2 cups sushi rice
      • 2 1/2 cups water
      • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
      • 2 tablespoons sugar
      • 1 teaspoon salt

    • For the Sushi Rolls:

      • 4 sheets nori
      • 200g fresh raw fish (like salmon or tuna) or cooked seafood
      • 1 cucumber (sliced into strips)
      • 1 avocado (sliced)
      • Pickled ginger, soy sauce, and wasabi (for serving)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Rice:

      Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Cook in a pot with water or a rice cooker. Once cooked, mix the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt, and fold it into the rice. Allow it to cool.


    • Assemble the Sushi Rolls:

      Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo mat. Spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border. Add fish, cucumber, and avocado in a line near one edge. Roll tightly using the bamboo mat.


    • Slice and Serve:

      Slice the roll into bite-sized pieces with a sharp knife. Serve with pickled ginger, soy sauce, and wasabi.

Tempura


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Tempura Batter:

      • 1 cup all-purpose flour
      • 1 cup ice-cold water
      • 1 large egg
      • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (optional)
      • Pinch of salt

    • For the Tempura:

      • 1 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
      • 1 cup assorted vegetables (e.g., sweet potato, zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers)
      • Oil for deep frying
      • Tempura dipping sauce (optional, store-bought or homemade)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Batter:

      In a bowl, beat the egg lightly. Add ice-cold water and mix. Gradually add flour, baking soda (if using), and salt. Stir gently until just combined; the batter should be slightly lumpy.


    • Prepare the Ingredients:

      Pat the shrimp and vegetables dry with a paper towel. Heat oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C).


    • Fry the Tempura:

      Dip the shrimp and vegetables into the batter, ensuring they are coated evenly. Fry in small batches until golden and crisp, about 2-3 minutes. Drain on paper towels.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot with tempura dipping sauce on the side.

Unagi


  • Ingredients:


    • For the Unagi:

      • 2 fillets of freshwater eel (unagi)
      • 1/4 cup soy sauce
      • 2 tablespoons mirin (sweet rice wine)
      • 2 tablespoons sugar
      • 1 tablespoon sake

    • For Serving:

      • Steamed white rice
      • Chopped green onions (optional)
      • Pickled ginger (optional)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Sauce:

      In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and sake. Cook over medium heat until slightly thickened, about 5 minutes. Set aside.


    • Cook the Eel:

      Preheat a grill or oven broiler. Brush the eel fillets with the prepared sauce and grill or broil until caramelized and heated through, about 4-5 minutes per side.


    • Serve:

      Place the cooked eel over steamed white rice. Drizzle with extra sauce and garnish with chopped green onions or pickled ginger if desired.

Yakitori


  • Ingredients:


    • For Yakitori:

      • 500g chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
      • 1/4 cup soy sauce
      • 1/4 cup mirin
      • 2 tablespoons sake
      • 2 tablespoons sugar
      • 1 bunch green onions (cut into 2-inch pieces)
      • Skewers (soaked in water if wooden)

  • Recipe:


    • Prepare the Sauce:

      In a saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until slightly thickened, about 10 minutes. Set aside.


    • Assemble the Skewers:

      Thread chicken and green onion alternately onto the skewers.


    • Grill the Yakitori:

      Preheat a grill or grill pan. Cook the skewers over medium heat, brushing with sauce and turning occasionally, until chicken is cooked through and lightly charred, about 8-10 minutes.


    • Serve:

      Serve hot as an appetizer or main dish, with extra sauce on the side if desired.
